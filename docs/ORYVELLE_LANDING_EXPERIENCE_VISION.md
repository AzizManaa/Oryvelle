# Oryvelle Landing Experience Vision

## Purpose

This document defines the larger landing experience for Oryvelle. It is inspired by the Android app's home, explore, and active playback visuals, but it should not become a direct copy of the app UI. The website should feel like an invitation into the same night world: mysterious, quiet, premium, and clean enough that the user trusts it before they fully understand it.

The goal is simple: make visitors curious enough to want the app.

Oryvelle should not feel like a normal marketing page with stacked feature sections and obvious sales copy. It should feel like entering a night ritual: a small, controlled, cinematic experience that reveals just enough to pull people forward.

## Core Feeling

Oryvelle is not loud wellness software. It is a dark, calm, precise companion for building softer nights.

The website should feel:

- Mysterious, but never confusing.
- Premium, but not cold.
- Celestial, but not sci-fi cosplay.
- Emotional, but not dramatic.
- Interactive, but not game-like.
- Clean, but not sterile.

The visitor should feel like they are seeing part of a larger system. The app should feel alive in the distance, not fully explained all at once.

## Android Visual Findings To Carry Forward

The strongest Android moments are:

- The home screen uses a large celestial orb as the emotional center of the product.
- The orb feels like a black-hole sleep object: dark center, soft teal bloom, thin orbital rings, tiny stars, and drifting sound satellites.
- The idle home state is spacious, with copy sitting above the orb and glass controls below.
- The active playback home state is much stronger than the idle state for marketing: small sound icons orbit the central orb, the bottom panel says `Calming Rain + 2 more`, and the whole scene feels alive.
- The Explore screen is a full-screen stellar sound map. It is visually interesting, but too dense to become the main website hero.
- The glass dock and active mix panels feel like product language: translucent surfaces, teal active states, rounded but not bubbly, soft borders, and restrained labels.

Landing-page inspiration should come mostly from the active home playback state: a giant central orb, sound satellites, glass overlays, and a feeling that the app is already creating a ritual.

## What We Should Not Copy

The website should not copy the Android app screen-for-screen.

Avoid:

- A fake phone mockup as the hero centerpiece.
- Literal Android navigation tabs on the website.
- A full recreation of the Explore canvas as the first impression.
- Dense feature cards explaining every capability.
- Generic app-store landing sections like "Track your sleep", "Relax faster", or "Personalized insights" without atmosphere.
- Medical claims or promises that Oryvelle improves sleep outcomes.

The website should borrow the soul, not the interface.

## Experience Concept

The landing page should behave like a single immersive stage with controlled reveals.

Instead of scrolling through normal sections, the user enters a night field. The orb becomes the guide. As the user scrolls, wheels, taps, or swipes, the scene shifts through product moments. The content changes around the orb, but the orb remains the anchor.

The page should feel like:

1. Arrival into the night.
2. Sounds quietly gathering around the orb.
3. A wind-down timer being set.
4. A morning note appearing as a soft echo.
5. A final invitation to download.

This can be implemented as one vertical page or a controlled full-screen ritual, but the feeling should not be a standard stacked landing page. Even if scrolling is used, sections should blend into each other like scene changes.

## Primary Landing Page Structure

### 1. Arrival

First viewport.

The visitor sees:

- A dark, spacious night field.
- The `Oryvelle` wordmark in a small translucent header.
- Disabled-looking trust labels such as `Privacy`, `Terms`, and `Support`.
- A subdued `Coming soon` or app-store CTA depending on launch state.
- A large immersive orb occupying the visual center.
- The exact core line: `Build a softer path into sleep.`
- Supporting copy that stays short and calm.

Suggested supporting copy:

`Layer calming sounds, set a gentle timer, and leave a small note for morning. Oryvelle keeps the ritual quiet.`

The orb should not sit inside a phone. It should feel almost full-screen, with the page content arranged around it.

Animation:

- The orb breathes slowly.
- Rings rotate at different speeds.
- Tiny particles drift around the outer glow.
- The starfield has barely visible movement.
- Pointer movement creates a soft parallax pull, not a flashy spotlight.

Reduced motion:

- The orb becomes mostly still.
- Rings may keep a barely visible static offset.
- Scene transitions become fade-only.

### 2. Sound Gathering

The orb shifts from solitary to active.

Small sound satellites appear around it:

- `Rain`
- `Cabin`
- `Forest`
- `Noise`

They should not look like ordinary cards. They should feel like small glass capsules or tiny planetary objects connected to the orb by faint orbital paths.

Copy direction:

`Sounds do not fight for attention. They gather into a mix you can shape quietly.`

Visual behavior:

- Sound satellites drift into orbit one by one.
- Their glow intensity suggests volume or balance.
- The orb's teal bloom grows slightly as the mix becomes active.
- A faint active-mix glass panel can appear near the bottom, inspired by the Android dock, but simplified for web.

CTA behavior:

- A soft secondary action can appear: `Hear the ritual` or `Preview the mix`, but it should not require audio playback unless we intentionally support it later.
- Primary download CTA remains restrained and persistent.

### 3. Wind-Down Timer

The scene becomes more precise.

The orb's orbital ring transforms into a timer arc. The page should suggest time passing without becoming a dashboard.

Copy direction:

`Set a timer, let the mix fade, and leave the phone alone.`

Visual behavior:

- A thin circular timer trace wraps around the orb.
- The trace slowly completes or dissolves.
- Sound satellites dim slightly as if the night is settling.
- A small label can show `45 min fade` or `Fade out`, but it should feel like a ritual cue, not a settings panel.

The timer moment should make Oryvelle feel useful without turning the page into a feature checklist.

### 4. Morning Note

The night field softens.

The orb becomes quieter and more distant. A small morning note appears as a clean glass surface, not a full analytics dashboard.

Copy direction:

`In the morning, leave only what matters. No pressure, no dashboard noise.`

Visual behavior:

- Star movement slows.
- The orb glow cools.
- A small note surface appears with a simple mood/rating gesture.
- The page should hint at reflection, not measurement obsession.

Important:

Do not overpromise personalization. The tone should be gentle and optional.

### 5. Final Pull

The final moment should not feel like a hard sell.

The orb can return to the center, now surrounded by the quiet traces of the previous moments: faint sound satellites, a timer ring, and a small morning note mark.

Copy direction:

`Start with one softer night.`

CTA direction:

- Pre-launch: `Coming soon`
- Launch: `Download on the App Store` and/or `Get it on Google Play`
- Optional: `Join the waitlist`

The final screen should feel like the door has opened. It should make the app feel desirable because the user has experienced its atmosphere, not because we listed everything it does.

## Supporting Pages

The site should eventually include supporting pages, but they should preserve the same visual system without becoming immersive for no reason.

### Download Page

Purpose:

Give users a direct place to download the app or join the waitlist.

Look:

- Clean dark page.
- Large orb cropped softly in the background.
- App store buttons or waitlist form.
- Short trust copy.
- Minimal FAQ.

Animation:

- Very subtle orb drift.
- Button hover glow.
- No complex scene transitions.

### Privacy Page

Purpose:

Build trust without theatrical design.

Look:

- Mostly text.
- Dark background with faint stars.
- Strong readability.
- Small Oryvelle wordmark.
- No giant animated orb behind legal text.

Tone:

Clear, plain, and reassuring.

Animation:

- None or nearly none.

### Terms Page

Purpose:

Legal clarity.

Look:

- Same structure as Privacy.
- High contrast text.
- Simple anchors for sections.

Animation:

- None.

### Support Page

Purpose:

Give users a calm way to get help.

Look:

- Dark clean layout.
- Small glass contact panel.
- Common questions grouped by topic.
- A subtle mini orb or star mark, not a full hero.

Animation:

- Gentle hover states only.

### Press / Brand Page

Purpose:

Optional future page for assets and brand story.

Look:

- More editorial than legal pages.
- Use the orb as an identity object.
- Include logo, app icon, screenshots, short product description, and contact.

Animation:

- Small reveal transitions.
- No heavy canvas unless needed.

## Navigation Model

The header should stay quiet and translucent.

Desktop:

- Left: `Oryvelle`
- Center or right: trust labels
- Right: CTA

Mobile:

- Left: `Oryvelle`
- Right: CTA or compact menu
- Trust links can move to a small bottom/legal area once real pages exist.

Before legal pages exist, trust labels must look disabled and must not route to missing pages.

## Orb Direction

The orb is the brand object.

It should feel:

- Deep, dark, and gravitational.
- Soft around the edges.
- Alive, but not aggressive.
- Slightly different in each scene.

Orb scene behavior:

- Arrival: large, centered, quiet.
- Sound Gathering: brighter teal edge, satellites appear.
- Timer: ring becomes more precise and circular.
- Morning Note: glow cools, motion slows.
- Final Pull: all marks combine into a composed ritual state.

The orb should not become decorative wallpaper. It should always carry meaning.

## Color Direction

Base colors:

- Near-black night.
- Deep blue-black.
- Muted indigo.
- Teal glow.
- Soft moon-white text.
- Warm yellow only for small path or ritual cues.

Avoid:

- Purple-blue gradient overload.
- Beige wellness palettes.
- Bright neon cyberpunk colors.
- Flat black without depth.
- Too many competing accent colors.

The Android app uses teal and purple well because the teal has a functional role: active sound, selected state, or living energy. The website should keep that discipline.

## Typography Direction

Typography should be calm and spacious.

Hero text:

- Large, but not shouty.
- Light or regular weight for emotional lines.
- Stronger weight only for the brand or CTA labels.

Supporting text:

- Short.
- Muted.
- Clear.

Avoid:

- Long paragraphs in the immersive page.
- Marketing buzzwords.
- Feature explanations that break the spell.

## Copy Principles

Oryvelle copy should invite, not insist.

Good:

- `Build a softer path into sleep.`
- `Layer the night quietly.`
- `Let the mix fade when you are ready.`
- `Leave a small note for morning.`
- `No pressure. No dashboard noise.`

Avoid:

- `Sleep better instantly.`
- `Science-backed sleep optimization.`
- `Unlock your perfect sleep score.`
- `Maximize your rest.`

The mystery comes from restraint. We should show just enough of the product ritual to make the user curious.

## Animation Principles

Animations should feel expensive because they are controlled.

Use:

- Slow breathing scale.
- Orbital drift.
- Subtle particle movement.
- Crossfade between scenes.
- Small parallax based on pointer position.
- Smooth rail/progress transitions.
- Low-count canvas elements.

Avoid:

- Fast loops.
- Confetti-like particles.
- Constant bouncing.
- Large blur layers that hurt performance.
- Animating too many DOM nodes.
- Scroll-jacking that traps the user.

Performance rule:

The page should look alive even when animation is reduced. The composition must carry the design, not just motion.

## Interaction Principles

The landing page should reward exploration without requiring it.

Desktop:

- Wheel or scroll shifts scenes.
- Keyboard arrows can move between moments.
- Progress rail allows direct jumps.
- Pointer movement softly affects the orb field.

Mobile:

- Swipe changes moments.
- Bottom rail remains reachable.
- CTA remains accessible.
- Text must never overlap the orb or dock.

The site should never feel like a puzzle. Mystery is visual and emotional, not navigational.

## Conversion Strategy

Oryvelle should pull users toward download by creating desire, not urgency.

Conversion moments:

- Persistent small CTA in header.
- CTA after sound satellites gather.
- CTA after timer moment.
- Final full invitation.

CTA tone:

- Pre-launch: `Coming soon`, `Join waitlist`, or `Notify me`
- Launch: `Download Oryvelle`

The CTA should feel calm and inevitable. It should not interrupt the ritual.

## Page-Level Animation Inspiration

### Landing Page

Most animated page.

Use the orb, satellites, starfield, and scene changes.

### Download Page

Subtle background orb only.

CTA buttons should feel physical and polished.

### Privacy / Terms

Mostly static.

Trust comes from clarity, not spectacle.

### Support

Small glass forms and hover states.

The page can include a faint starfield, but no immersive interaction.

### 404 Page

Can be atmospheric.

Concept:

`This part of the night is quiet.`

Use a small distant orb, one CTA back home, and no heavy animation.

## Accessibility And Reduced Motion

The immersive page must remain accessible.

Requirements:

- All progress controls are buttons.
- Scene text exists as readable HTML.
- Keyboard navigation works.
- Focus states are visible.
- Reduced motion disables heavy animation.
- Canvas content is decorative unless paired with text.
- CTA remains reachable without interacting with the scene.

Reduced motion should not make the page feel broken. It should become a still cinematic poster with clean fades.

## Technical Direction

Keep the site performant.

Use:

- Server-rendered page structure.
- One focused client component for the immersive landing experience.
- Canvas for the orb/starfield.
- CSS transforms and opacity for scene changes.
- No new animation dependencies unless there is a strong reason.

Avoid:

- Heavy image/video backgrounds for the core scene.
- Large Lottie files.
- Multiple competing canvases.
- Fake screenshots.
- Auto-playing audio.

The app download site should be light enough to feel instant.

## Success Criteria

The landing experience succeeds if:

- The first viewport makes the user pause.
- The orb feels like a unique Oryvelle brand object.
- The page does not look like a template landing page.
- The product feels useful without being overexplained.
- The website makes the app feel mysterious but trustworthy.
- The CTA feels natural, not forced.
- The design remains readable and performant on mobile.

## North Star

The website should feel like the first night with Oryvelle, not a brochure about Oryvelle.

It should make the visitor think:

`I do not know everything this app does yet, but I want to enter that space.`
